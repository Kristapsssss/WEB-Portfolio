UK Online Gambling Industry Analysis

Data Source

https://www.gamblingcommission.gov.uk/statistics-and-research/publication/market-overview-operator-data-to-march-2024-published-april-2024

Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of the UK online gambling industry from 2021 to 2023. Using data sourced from UK's gambling commission, we examine player activity, betting behavior, financial performance, and seasonal trends across different gambling verticals.

Data Overview

Data Collection: Data was sourced from quarterly reports provided by major online gambling operators in the UK. The dataset includes metrics such as active players, number of bets, session lengths, and Gross Gaming Yield (GGY) across multiple gambling categories, including Slots, Casino, Betting (Real event and Virtual), Poker, and eSports.

Technologies Used: The project utilized Python for data processing and analysis. Key libraries included pandas for data manipulation, matplotlib and seaborn for data visualization, and Flask for building the web application.

Key Findings

  • Active Players: The total number of active players increased steadily from 2021 to 2023, with Slots showing the highest growth (25.14% over three years).
  • Betting Behavior: Real event betting consistently had the highest number of bets and GGY, followed by Slots and Casino.
  • Session Length: Average session lengths decreased slightly from 20 minutes (2021) to 19 minutes (2023), while the number of sessions longer than 1 hour increased, indicating more prolonged engagement.
  • Profitability: Slots and Real event betting were the most profitable verticals in terms of Gross Gaming Yield (GGY).

Trends and Analysis

Active Players (2021-2023)

  • Slots: Active players increased from 37,848,866 in 2021 to 47,363,536 in 2023, showing a growth of 25.14%.
  • Other gaming (including casino): Active players increased from 26,320,459 in 2021 to 30,385,198 in 2023, showing a growth of 15.44%.
  • Betting (Real event): Active players increased from 62,800,852 in 2021 to 67,030,586 in 2023, showing a growth of 6.74%.
  • Betting (Virtual): Active players decreased from 2,855,769 in 2021 to 2,336,382 in 2023, showing a decline of 18.19%.
  • Poker: Active players decreased from 3,459,175 in 2021 to 2,756,170 in 2023, showing a decline of 20.32%.

Player Activity Share (2021-2023)

  • Slots: Increased from 28.40% (2021) to 31.60% (2023).
  • Other gaming: Slight decrease from 19.75% (2021) to 20.27% (2023).
  • Betting (Real event): Decreased from 47.12% (2021) to 44.73% (2023).
  • Betting (Virtual): Declined from 2.14% (2021) to 1.56% (2023).
  • Poker: Decreased from 2.60% (2021) to 1.84% (2023).

Seasonal Trends (Monthly Averages)

  • Slots: Highest activity in December, indicating a seasonal spike during the holiday period. Monthly averages ranged from 3,334,527 in February to 3,837,130 in December.
  • Betting (Real event): Peaks in March and April, likely due to major sports events. Monthly averages ranged from 4,671,974 in July to 7,225,841 in April.
  • Other gaming (Casino): Significant spikes in March and December, potentially due to seasonal promotions or events. Monthly averages ranged from 2,113,683 in February to 2,532,339 in December.

Session Length and Long Sessions

The average session length has decreased from 20 minutes (2021) to 19 minutes (2023). However, the number of sessions longer than 1 hour has increased from 2,887,975 in 2021 to 3,108,490 in 2023, indicating a trend towards more prolonged engagement among players.

Gross Gaming Yield (GGY) Trends

  • Slots: Increased from €2,297,386,624 (2021) to €2,341,352,883 (2023), with a total increase of 2.04%.
  • Casino: Decreased from €750,640,546 (2021) to €587,872,732 (2023), with a total decrease of 22.68%.
  • Betting (Real event): Decreased from €2,534,250,872 (2021) to €1,989,756,201 (2023), with a total decrease of 19.83%.
  • Betting (Virtual): Decreased from €78,563,342 (2021) to €43,722,102 (2023), with a total decrease of 47.99%.
  • Poker: Decreased from €89,297,262 (2021) to €65,649,859 (2023), with a total decrease of 28.36%.

Most Profitable Vertical (2021-2023 Totals)

  • Slots: The most profitable vertical with a total GGY of €2,341,352,883 in 2023, showing consistent growth across the years.
  • Betting (Real event): The second most profitable vertical with a total GGY of €1,989,756,201 in 2023, despite some fluctuations.

Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) and Profit Per Bet

  • Slots (2023): ARPU was €49.43. Profit per bet was €0.03.
  • Casino (2023): ARPU was €19.59. Profit per bet was €0.15.
  • Betting (Real event) (2023): ARPU was €29.87. Profit per bet was €0.53.
  • Betting (Virtual) (2023): ARPU was €18.71. Profit per bet was €0.38.
  • Poker (2023): ARPU was €23.82. Profit per bet was €0.08.

Most Profitable Bet Types (2023)

  • Betting (Real event): With an ARPU of €29.87 and a profit per bet of €0.53, real event betting stands out as a high revenue-generating category.
  • Slots: Despite having a lower ARPU of €49.43 compared to previous years, slots still generate consistent profits with a profit per bet of €0.03 due to high volume of bets placed.
  • Casino: With an ARPU of €19.59 and a profit per bet of €0.15, casino games remain a steady source of revenue.
  • Betting (Virtual): Although having a lower ARPU of €18.71, the profit per bet of €0.38 indicates efficiency in generating revenue from virtual bets.
  • Poker: With an ARPU of €23.82 and a profit per bet of €0.08, poker remains a profitable but less significant vertical compared to others.

Yearly Trends in Gross Gaming Yield (GGY)

  • 2021: Total GGY was €5,784,650,963. Highest GGY in Slots with €2,297,386,624, followed by Betting (Real event) with €2,534,250,872.
  • 2022: Total GGY was €4,871,299,086. Slots remained the highest with €2,235,208,089, followed by Betting (Real event) with €1,871,451,767.
  • 2023: Total GGY was €5,048,289,094. Slots again led with €2,341,352,883, followed by Betting (Real event) with €1,989,756,201.

Conclusion and Recommendations

Key Findings

  • The UK online gambling industry experienced steady growth in active players from 2021 to 2023, particularly in the Slots and real event betting verticals.
  • Real event betting and Slots were the most profitable verticals, though Casino, Virtual betting, and Poker saw declines in GGY.
  • Seasonal trends highlighted peaks in player activity during certain months, particularly in December for Slots and March-April for real event betting.

Recommendations

  • Leverage seasonal trends: Operators should offer targeted promotions during peak months to maximize engagement and revenue.
  • Focus on profitable verticals: Focus on Slots and real event betting to drive profitability, while exploring strategies to revitalize interest in Casino and Poker.
  • Enhance user engagement: Understand the reasons behind the increase in long sessions and develop features or promotions that encourage prolonged play.
  • Monitor regulatory changes: Stay ahead of regulatory changes and market conditions to adapt strategies and maintain compliance while optimizing profitability.

Future Outlook

The online gambling industry is likely to continue evolving with technological advancements and changing player preferences. Operators should stay ahead of trends by investing in innovative technologies and personalized gaming experiences to attract and retain players.
