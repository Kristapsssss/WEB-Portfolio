UK Online Gambling Industry Analysis

Summary

This report provides a detailed analysis of the UK online gambling industry from 2021 to 2023, based on data from the UK's Gambling Commission. Key findings indicate growth in active players and profitability in Slots and Real Event Betting verticals, alongside seasonal trends and recommendations for future strategies.

Data Source

Gambling Commission Market Overview

Technologies and Skills Demonstrated

Technologies Used: Excel

Skills Demonstrated: Data analysis

Key Findings

Active Players: Increased steadily from 2021 to 2023, with Slots showing the highest growth (25.14%).

Betting Behavior: Real event betting had the highest number of bets and GGY, followed by Slots and Casino.

Session Length: Average session lengths decreased slightly, but sessions longer than 1 hour increased.

Profitability: Slots and Real event betting were the most profitable verticals in terms of GGY.

Trends and Analysis

Active Players (2021-2023)

Slots: Active players increased from 37,848,866 in 2021 to 47,363,536 in 2023 (25.14% growth).

Other gaming (including casino): Active players increased from 26,320,459 in 2021 to 30,385,198 in 2023 (15.44% growth).

Betting (Real event): Active players increased from 62,800,852 in 2021 to 67,030,586 in 2023 (6.74% growth).

Betting (Virtual): Active players decreased from 2,855,769 in 2021 to 2,336,382 in 2023 (18.19% decline).

Poker: Active players decreased from 3,459,175 in 2021 to 2,756,170 in 2023 (20.32% decline).

Player Activity Share (2021-2023)

Slots: Increased from 28.40% (2021) to 31.60% (2023).

Other gaming: Slight increase from 19.75% (2021) to 20.27% (2023).

Betting (Real event): Decreased from 47.12% (2021) to 44.73% (2023).

Betting (Virtual): Declined from 2.14% (2021) to 1.56% (2023).

Poker: Decreased from 2.60% (2021) to 1.84% (2023).

Seasonal Trends (Monthly Averages)

Slots: Highest activity in December. Monthly averages ranged from 3,334,527 in February to 3,837,130 in December.

Betting (Real event): Peaks in March and April. Monthly averages ranged from 4,671,974 in July to 7,225,841 in April.

Other gaming (Casino): Spikes in March and December. Monthly averages ranged from 2,113,683 in February to 2,532,339 in December.

Session Length and Long Sessions

Average session length: Decreased from 22 minutes (2021) to 18 minutes (2023). However, sessions longer than 1 hour increased, indicating more prolonged engagement.

Gross Gaming Yield (GGY) Trends

Slots: Increased from €2,297,386,624 (2021) to €2,341,352,883 (2023), a 2.04% increase.

Casino: Decreased from €750,640,546 (2021) to €587,872,732 (2023), a 22.68% decrease.

Betting (Real event): Decreased from €2,534,250,872 (2021) to €1,989,756,201 (2023), a 19.83% decrease.

Betting (Virtual): Decreased from €78,563,342 (2021) to €43,722,102 (2023), a 47.99% decrease.

Poker: Decreased from €89,297,262 (2021) to €65,649,859 (2023), a 28.36% decrease.

Most Profitable Vertical (2021-2023 Totals)

Slots: Most profitable with a GGY of €2,341,352,883 in 2023, showing consistent growth.

Betting (Real event): Second most profitable with a GGY of €1,989,756,201 in 2023, despite fluctuations.

Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) and Profit Per Bet

Slots (2023): ARPU was €49.43, the highest among all verticals, indicating that players spend more on average in this category.

Casino (2023): ARPU was €19.59, showing moderate spending per user.

Betting (Real event) (2023): ARPU was €29.87, reflecting substantial average spending per user. Profit per bet was €0.53, the highest profit per bet among all categories.

Betting (Virtual) (2023): ARPU was €18.71, indicating lower average spending per user.

Poker (2023): ARPU was €23.82, demonstrating reasonable average spending per user.

Most Profitable Bet Types (2023)

Betting (Real event): With an ARPU of €29.87 and a profit per bet of €0.53, real event betting stands out.

Slots: Despite a lower ARPU of €49.43, slots generate consistent profits with a profit per bet of €0.03 due to high volume.

Casino: ARPU of €19.59 and profit per bet of €0.15, making casino games a steady revenue source.

Betting (Virtual): ARPU of €18.71 and profit per bet of €0.38 indicate efficient revenue generation.

Poker: ARPU of €23.82 and profit per bet of €0.08, profitable but less significant compared to other verticals.

Yearly Trends in Gross Gaming Yield (GGY)

2021: Total GGY was €5,784,650,963. Highest in Slots with €2,297,386,624, followed by Betting (Real event) with €2,534,250,872.

2022: Total GGY was €4,871,299,086. Slots remained highest with €2,235,208,089, followed by Betting (Real event) with €1,871,451,767.

2023: Total GGY was €5,048,289,094. Slots led with €2,341,352,883, followed by Betting (Real event) with €1,989,756,201.

Conclusion and Recommendations

Key Findings

The UK online gambling industry experienced steady growth in active players from 2021 to 2023, particularly in Slots and real event betting.

Real event betting and Slots were the most profitable verticals, though Casino, Virtual betting, and Poker saw declines in GGY.

Seasonal trends highlighted peaks in player activity during certain months, particularly in December for Slots and March-April for real event betting.

Recommendations

Seasonal Promotions: Leverage seasonal peaks by offering special promotions during December (for Slots) and March-April (for Real Event Betting) to maximize player engagement and revenue.

Focus on profitable verticals: Focus on Slots and real event betting to drive profitability, while exploring strategies to revitalize interest in Casino and Poker.

Enhance user engagement: Understand the reasons behind the increase in long sessions and develop features or promotions that encourage prolonged play.

Personalization: Use data analytics to personalize user experiences, offering tailored game recommendations and promotions based on individual player behavior and preferences.

Churn Analysis: Identify patterns in player churn and develop targeted retention strategies to minimize player attrition.
